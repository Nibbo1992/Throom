Origin: The Flaw Line

Understanding Throom begins with the story of his failure and exile, which informs his absolute self-imposed code of duty and dishonor.

The Flaw Line

Tholai was born into the Ogolakanu Clan, a singular Goliath community whose faith revolved around absolute, unmoving Serenity. As an apprentice stoneworker, he was taught that chaos was a betrayal of the spirit and that true strength was measured by unyielding peace. Yet, Tholai carried a terrifying, primal flaw: an uncontrollable Rage that felt like a flaw line in sacred stone. He struggled daily to contain the fury, knowing his very existence was an existential threat to his clan's beliefs.

Stonebreaker

The calamity came when raiders threatened the peaks. Tholai, losing control, unleashed his Rage. His fury crushed the enemy and saved his clan, but it simultaneously shattered the sacred silence and the stonework of his ancestral home. The Elders saw not a hero, but a spiritual failure—a contagion of chaos. The Chieftain gave him the shaming nickname "Stonebreaker" and cast him out, deeming him a liability whose reliance on brute force was a betrayal of their survival creed.

Exiled and dishonored, Tholai rejected his full name. He adopted the blunt, simple moniker "Throom," choosing a sound that was empty of honor or history, befitting a man who saw himself as a mere weapon—a self-imposed punishment that affirmed his tragic code: "My strength is weakness. My serenity is rage. My duty is dishonor".

The Quest for the Purest Evil

Throom wandered the lands, using his terrifying strength only to uphold the duty he could not fulfill among his own kin: the protection of the innocent. However, Throom realized that helping common folk was insufficient penance; it did not justify the inherent dishonor of his existence. He became obsessed with a singular, terrible purpose: finding an evil so profound, so utterly corrupting, that his own contaminated power would be the only fitting weapon. He sought out Barovia, viewing its dark master, Strahd, as the ultimate problem that requires the ultimate, violent solution—The Purest Evil. He seeks to use his Rage to shatter this despair, offering his own destruction as the final, necessary sacrifice to achieve ultimate, paradoxical redemption.

Presence & Communication

Throom's character is conveyed almost entirely through his physical presence and minimalist communication. This section provides a reference for how he moves, fights, and speaks (or doesn't speak).

Physicality: The Living Wall

General Movement

Defined by **weight and resistance.** He shifts his mass; he does not run. His boots grind the earth. Always interposes his body between threat and ally.

The Greataxe (Combat)

A tool of **necessary chaos and shame.** Blows must shatter or annihilate. He retrieves the weapon with a sigh of discipline, not a cry of victory.

Environmental Tension

He must **fix a flawed reality.** In moments of stress, he imposes order: forcefully reseating a loose stone, neatly stacking firewood, or using his Mason's Chisel to smooth a jagged edge. Justified with tactical language: "A perimeter is clean."

Voice: The Weight of Duty

Tone & Delivery

Low, measured, gravelly, heavy. Zero lightness or irony. Sentences are unavoidable facts. (e.g., asked how he is: "Sufficient.")

Minimalist Commands

Specific, directive, location-based: "Behind." "Hold ground." "To the stone." He asks only "Where?" and "What?", never "Why?" or "How?".

The Grunt ($\mu$)

His primary response to kindness or compliments. A low, troubled, wordless sound of deep discomfort, immediately followed by a pivot to manual labor to "repay" the debt.

Rage Protocol: The Necessary Failure

For Throom, Rage is not a source of power but a moment of profound shame—a surrender of the discipline he values above all. This protocol outlines the three phases of this critical internal battle.

PHASE 1

Pre-Rage (The Struggle)

He stands utterly still. Knuckles turn white on his weapon. He sucks in a pained, ragged breath that sounds like tearing cloth. This is him fighting the surrender.
